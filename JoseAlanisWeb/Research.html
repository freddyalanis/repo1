Research Projects and Mathematical Notes.

Topography and Behavior Based Movement Modeling for Missing Hikers in Land-Wilderness Settings

This paper explores a mathematical model for Search and Rescue (SAR) operations, incorporating human behavior to improve lost person predictions. Using real incident data and a Markov Decision Process, it enhances SAR efficiency and survival outcomes. Find more here.

Modeling Climate Change’s Impact on Macrocystis Pyrifera Through Population Dynamics

A mathematical model predicts the impact of climate change on giant kelp populations along the California coast. The study analyzes how rising sea temperatures threaten kelp survival, with projections of severe decline by 2080. Find more here.

Computational Methods for Electrical Impedance Tomography

Electrical Impedance Tomography (EIT) is a low-cost, portable imaging system with applications in medicine and industry. This study explores deep neural network approaches to improve EIT’s challenging inverse problem, enhancing resolution and computational efficiency. Find more here.

Weaving through Incompleteness in Logical Thought and it’s Aftermath

Mathematical logic is critical for scientific advancement. However, despite its rigor, not all true statements can be proven, revealing the inherent limits of mathematical knowledge. Find more here.
